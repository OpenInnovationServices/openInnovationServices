Fergal Brophy

Fergal is Entrepreneurial Specialist at the Innovation Academy, University College Dublin and Founder/MD at Open Innovation Services. His focus is on facilitating and mentoring innovation, creativity and entrepreneurship for Corporates, SMEs and the undergrad/postdoc startup community. He has advised on a wide range of innovation initiatives at ESB, MetLife, Deloitte, PwC, BDO, Roche Pharma, Primark, Bank of Ireland, New Ireland Assurance, Enterprise Ireland, Irish Stock Exchange, Health Research Board (HRB), US Embassy and numerous SMEs. Fergal is lead facilitator for the Innovation Pillar on Enterprise Ireland’s Go Global 4 Growth management development program and is a mentor to awardees of the not-for-profit Social Innovation Fund (SIF). He has extensive commercial experience in South-East Asia and China. Fergal co-founded Open Financial Services, a fintech company, in 2001, with many Fortune 500 clients, which was scaled and sold to GreatWest Life, one of World’s largest financial services firms in 2014.

Deirdre Smith

Deirdre is a highly experienced and respected director, coach and mentor who has worked with multiple clients over the last 25 years. Deirdre co-founded Zandar Technologies in 1993 and sold it in 2007 to Harris Corporation, an S&P 500 company. Zandar developed multi-viewer solutions for the broadcasting industry. Deirdre became Director, Product Line Management at Harris where she led a multidisciplinary, multi-location innovation team. She started her own management consultancy in 2010 which focused on lean, innovation and team coaching. Deirdre is a mentor on Go Global 4 Growth, an Enterprise Ireland (EI) programme designed to challenge CEOs and CFOs of SME’s in the technology sector to scale. Deirdre holds a number of high-profile Non-Executive Directorships including NSAI and the Wellcome Trust HRB Clinical Research Facility jointly governed by St. James Hospital and Trinity College Dublin.

Dr. Declan Bogan

Declan is a senior independent business consultant who has been immersed in innovation across product-people-business model over the last 20 years. He has worked on innovation projects across multiple organisations in the life sciences sector operating in diverse therapeutic and device areas. From the bench to the boardroom, with micro to multinational organisations, Declan has worked internationally with intrapreneurs at J&J, Pfizer, Abbott, West Pharmaceuticals and Teleflex to name but a few. He brings this depth and breadth of knowledge as he manages client journeys through active project engagements, project execution and driving strategic momentum. He brings his skills in workshop facilitation, learning by doing, co-creation, coaching and mentoring. Declan also mentors startup awardees on ClimateKIC, Europe's largest public-private partnership focused on climate innovation to create a sustainable future for all.

Michael Naughton

Michael is Director of Legal Services Innovation at Cisco Systems where he works with small teams to design and deliver automated and systemised next-generation professional services. Cisco pioneered electronic signature and contract management systems and has transformed in-house legal service delivery through its Global Center of Excellence, a “can-do” team of 40 legal professionals who provide structured and measurable legal support. This approach has delivered better customer service, accelerated revenue, and generated savings in excess of $30 million in legal costs over the past five years.
